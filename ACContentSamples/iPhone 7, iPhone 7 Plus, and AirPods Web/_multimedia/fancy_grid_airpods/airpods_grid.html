Set Up
Click each item in the list below to learn more about setting up AirPods. Click a revealed image for a closer look.
To set up AirPods, open the lid of the charging case. The LED flashes white to indicate that the device is in setup mode.
Ensure that your unlocked iPhone is near the charging case, typically within two inches (5 cm). iOS displays a picture of the device prompting you to connect with the device.
You do not need to set up devices individually to work with AirPods. All supported devices signed in with your iCloud account can access AirPods. To access AirPods on your devices, tap the button.
To remove AirPods from individual devices, you can forget the pairing from the Bluetooth menu. To remove AirPods from all of your devices signed in to iCloud, press and hold the setup button until the LED flashes amber.

Use
Click each item in the list below to learn how to use AirPods. Click a revealed image for a closer look.
You can use AirPods with iPhone, Mac, and Apple Watch. After AirPods are set up, removing AirPods from the charging case automatically connects AirPods to the last used device.
AirPods detect when they are in your ears and automatically pause playback if you remove them.
You can use AirPods individually or as a pair. When you use AirPods as a pair and remove one from your ear, playback pauses.
When using AirPods, playback pauses for incoming calls.
AirPods simultaneously connect to both iPhone and Apple Watch. When active, AirPods always tries to connect to the last device on which AirPods were used.

Siri
Click each item in the list below to learn how Siri works with AirPods. Click a revealed image for a closer look.
When using AirPods, double-tapping one of your Airpods activates Siri. In addition to normal Siri functions, Siri can adjust volume, check the current battery level, and announce incoming callers.
Double-tapping one of your AirPods activates Siri by default. You can change what double-tapping does from Settings > Bluetooth. Double-tapping one of your AirPods can either activate Siri or play/pause playback.
You can also control microphones. The Microphone setting controls which microphone is active while you are using AirPods. If you set an individual microphone as the active microphone, it always operates as the microphone. Choosing Automatically Switch AirPods from the Microphone screen switches the microphone depending on which of the AirPods are in your ears.

Battery and Charging
Click each item in the list below to learn about AirPods and charging case batteries. Click a revealed image for a closer look.
AirPods have no power button. Simply remove AirPods from the charging case, and AirPods start working immediately.
You can charge AirPods by returning them to their charging case. While in the case, AirPods are in Airplane Mode. AirPods continually charge while in the charging case, even if the charging case isn't connected to a power source.

LED
The charging case LED can be one of three colors. Click each item in the list below to learn about the LED and what each color means. Click a revealed image for a closer look.
A flashing white light indicates AirPods are in setup mode. To manually set up AirPods with non-iCloud devices, put the charging case into setup mode by pressing and holding the setup button.
With AirPods stored inside the charging case, a green light indicates that AirPods are charged. With AirPods removed from the charging case, a green light indicates that the case is charged.
With AirPods stored inside the charging case, an amber light indicates that AirPods are charging. With AirPods removed from the charging case, an amber light indicates that the case is charging.
No light indicates that the case has no power and must be charged.

Troubleshooting
Click each item in the list below to learn more about basic troubleshooting steps. Click a revealed image for a closer look.
Ensure that the lid of the charging case is open, Bluetooth is active on your iOS device, and that AirPods are within range.
Put AirPods into the charging case and close the lid. Wait approximately 15 seconds.
Reopen the lid and check the LED. If the light flashes white, the device is in setup mode.
If the LED is green or amber, AirPods are ready for use. If the LED doesn't flash, you might need to connect the charging case to a power source and charge it.
Finally, you can reset AirPods to factory settings. To reset AirPods, press and hold the setup button on the back of the charging case until the light flashes amber. Advise customers that the button is flush with the case, so it might be difficult to see.
